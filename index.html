About Me

I fell in love with writing when I was a teenager and decided to combine it with my obsession: sports. My career as a sports reporter began in 2019 at The Business Standard, and for the next five years, I spent my days covering cricket and football in Bangladesh, working for NewsBangla24.com, The Business Post, and New Age. Even before I completed my undergraduate degree, I had become the de facto Sports Editor of The Business Post, resulting in a running joke in the industry that I was the youngest to do so in the country.

After gathering experience in reporting, copy-editing, and managing a team, I moved to the US in 2024 to pursue an MA in Journalism at the University of Mississippi, which I completed with a 4.0 GPA in 2026, gaining expertise in narrative long-form writing, multimedia production, and academic research, as I worked as a Graduate Assistant for the School of Journalism and New Media and as a Reporting Intern at The Oxford Eagle in my first year, and then in my second year, I worked for the Marketing and Communications Department, where I produced Campus Insights interviews for the Associated Press.

Moving forward, I hope to use my skills and abilities to report on government accountability and social responsibility, as I believe it is of paramount importance that the media continues to remain nonpartisan and make sure that it fulfills its responsibility towards the people, instead of focusing on what generates the most cash flow.
